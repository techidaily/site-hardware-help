## Mastering Personalized Volume Controls: Tips for Tuning Each App's Sound on Your Samsung Phone

### Key Takeaways

* Samsung Galaxy devices have a feature called "Sound Assistant" that allows you to control the volume of individual apps, which is not commonly found on other smartphones.
* To access this feature, download "Good Lock" from the Galaxy Store and open the "Life Up" tab. From there, download and open the "Sound Assistant" add-on.
* In the "Sound Assistant" settings, select "Individual App Volumes" to add the apps you want to control. You can adjust their volumes from this screen or customize the volume panel for easier access.

 Windows PCs allow you to [adjust the volume for specific apps](https://youtube-data.techidaily.com/24517605-new-2024-approved-buy-subscribers-wisely-watch-your-numbers-soar/), but smartphones don't have the same functionality. That is unless you have a Samsung Galaxy device and you know where to look. We'll show you.

 "[Good Lock](https://extra-skills.techidaily.com/in-2024-metaverse-mastery-rapidly-assemble-your-digital-self/)" is a suite of add-ons that can extend the functionality of your Samsung Galaxy device. The aptly named "Sound Assistant" add-on is all about customizing audio-related features. One of its best features is allowing you to control the volume of individual apps.

 The first thing to do is [download Good Lock](https://shop-links.co/link/?exclusive=1&publisher_slug=itechdaily19598&url=https%3A%2F%2Fgalaxystore.samsung.com%2Fdetail%2Fcom.samsung.android.goodlock%3FlangCd%3Den) from the Galaxy Store. After it's installed, open it and go to the "Life Up" tab. This is where you'll see "Sound Assistant," and you can tap the download icon.

![Tap the download icon.](https://static1.howtogeekimages.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2023/10/screenshot_20231002_100537_good-lock.jpg) 

 That will take you to the download page for the add-on in the Galaxy Store. Tap "Install" to proceed.

![Tap "Install."](https://static1.howtogeekimages.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2023/10/screenshot_20231002_100548_galaxy-store.jpg) 

 Now you can go back to Good Lock and open "Sound Assistant."

![Go to "Sound Assistant."](https://static1.howtogeekimages.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2023/10/screenshot_20231002_100933_good-lock.jpg) 

 Select "Individual App Volumes."

![Select "Individual App Volumes."](https://static1.howtogeekimages.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2023/10/screenshot_20231002_100939_sound-assistant.jpg) 

 We'll start by adding the apps you'd like to control individually. Tap the floating plus button in the bottom right corner.

![Tap the floating plus button in the bottom right corner.](https://static1.howtogeekimages.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2023/10/screenshot_20231002_100952_sound-assistant.jpg) 

 Select all the apps you want to control and tap "Add."

![Select apps and tap "Add."](https://static1.howtogeekimages.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2023/10/screenshot_20231002_101007_sound-assistant.jpg)
