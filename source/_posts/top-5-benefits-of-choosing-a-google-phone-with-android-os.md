##  Avoid Spam and Robocalls

![Turn on "See caller and spam ID."](https://static1.howtogeekimages.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/screenshot_20230907-135839.png) 

 Spam and robocalls are some of the most annoying byproducts of having a phone in your pocket all the time. The Google Phone app has a [couple of features specifically intended to combat this.](https://smart-video-creator.techidaily.com/unhappy-with-windows-10-photos-check-out-these-8-fantastic-alternatives/) 

 Google works with businesses to verify caller's identities and show the reason for calling. Even if you don't have a company's phone number in your contact list, Google can show you the name, making it easier to spot legitimate calls. The app can also automatically block calls that it suspects are spam.

##  Personalized Search Results

![Personalized search results.](https://static1.howtogeekimages.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/screenshot_20230907-150446.png) 

 As a Google product, you would assume the Google Phone app to include some powerful search features. One such feature is the ability to see personal results when searching in the Phone app.

 When you enable "Personalized Search Results," the app will pull in items from the search history on your Google account. Those results will appear when you do similar searches in the Phone app. To enable this feature, go to Settings > Nearby Places.

 A lot of the phone calls you make to businesses and other establishments are probably local. Wouldn't it make sense if your phone knew that? That's exactly where the "Nearby Places" feature comes in.

 Let's say you want to order some coffee. You can do a search for the shop's name, and the app will automatically know your location and show you the numbers for nearby coffee shops. You don't even need to bother opening [Google Maps](https://location-social.techidaily.com/how-to-detect-and-stop-mspy-from-spying-on-your-oppo-a1x-5g-drfone-by-drfone-virtual-android/). To enable this feature, go to Settings > Nearby Places. Also, make sure to enable the location permission.

##  Hear Who's Calling

![Choose one of the announcement types.](https://static1.howtogeekimages.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/screenshot_20230907-152833.png) 

 Smartphones make it a lot easier to see who's calling, but what if you want to hear who's calling? The Google Phone app has a feature for that too. It's called "[Caller ID Announcement](https://some-approaches.techidaily.com/new-top-5-methods-to-record-high-quality-audio-on-windows/)."

 You have a few options with this feature. It can announce the caller all the time or only when you're using a headset. The perfect solution for those times when you can't sneak a peek at your phone.

 If you're like a lot of people, you don't particularly enjoy making and receiving calls. Features like those found in the Google phone app can make the experience slightly more enjoyable. If you have a phone in your pocket, it shouldn't bother you.
