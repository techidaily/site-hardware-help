##  Recover a Deleted Photo From Google Photos

 If you're scrolling through your photo gallery and accidentally delete a photo instead of hitting share, the first place you'll want to look is the trash. Almost all Android phones have a trash bin that'll temporarily save content for a specified amount of time. Many devices ship with [Google Photos](https://www.anrdoezrs.net/links/3607085/type/dlg/sid/UUhtgUeUpU2000969/https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.google.android.apps.photos&hl=en%5FUS&gl=US) as the stock photo gallery app, and it's also what many people use for cloud backups.

 Open Google Photos and go to the "Library" tab.

![Google Photos library button](https://static1.howtogeekimages.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2023/11/google-photos-library-1.jpg) 

Cory Gunther / How-To Geek

 Select the "Trash" button with the garbage bin icon.

![Google Photos app trash button.](https://static1.howtogeekimages.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2023/11/google-photos-library-trash-1.jpg) 

Cory Gunther / How-To Geek

 Select a photo (or press and hold for multiple) and tap the "Restore" button.

![Google Photos restore deleted photo button](https://static1.howtogeekimages.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2023/11/google-photos-restore-deleted.jpg)

 Finally, if you're looking to recover photos from an Android phone that was recently erased (factory data reset), things get more challenging. If the files are [backed up to Google Photos](https://extra-approaches.techidaily.com/in-2024-no-cost-humor-in-your-arsenal-ultimate-meme-guide/), you can head into settings and restore a backup.  Hopefully one of these methods helps you recover deleted photos on your Android phone or tablet. And remember, having a backup is always a good idea.
