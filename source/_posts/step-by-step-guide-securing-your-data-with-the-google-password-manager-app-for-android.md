## Step-by-Step Guide: Securing Your Data with the Google Password Manager App for Android

A password manager is a great thing to use if you want to keep your passwords secure and easy to remember. [Google's Password Manager](https://mondly-stories.techidaily.com/the-complete-set-of-romance-language-nations/) built-in to Chrome is a popular option and it works on Android too.

 While we don't necessarily recommend everyone [use their browser's password manager](https://tech-revival.techidaily.com/perspectives-of-world-renowned-tech-experts-the-future-of-ai-according-to-them/), it's better than nothing. Plus, Google's password manager has gotten a lot better over the years. If you use it in Google Chrome on your computer, you should use it on Android, too. We'll show you how.

Related: [Why You Shouldn't Use Your Web Browser's Password Manager](https://tech-revival.techidaily.com/perspectives-of-world-renowned-tech-experts-the-future-of-ai-according-to-them/) 

 Before we get started, we'll need to make sure you have Google's password manager [set as your "Autofill" service](https://howto.techidaily.com/8-workable-fixes-to-the-sim-not-provisioned-mm2-error-on-nokia-c12-plus-drfone-by-drfone-fix-android-problems-fix-android-problems/) on your Android device. This will ensure that the passwords you save will automatically generate when you need to log in to an app or website.

 Swipe down once or twice (depending on your phone) from the top of the screen and tap the gear icon to open the Settings.

![Tap the gear icon.](https://static1.howtogeekimages.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2021/06/2021-06-10_16-45-27.png?trim=1,1&amp;bg-color=000&amp;pad=1,1)

 Scroll down to the "Passwords & Accounts" section. On a Samsung Galaxy device, you'll need to go to General Management > Passwords and Autofill.

![Tap the "Passwords & Accounts" section.](https://static1.howtogeekimages.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2022/02/2022-02-24_16-50-54.jpg) 

 Now select whichever service is listed under "Autofill Service." It may already be "Google."

![Select the service under "Autofill service."](https://static1.howtogeekimages.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2022/02/2022-02-24_16-51-35.jpg) 

 Choose "Google" from the list if it's not already selected.

![Choose "Google."](https://static1.howtogeekimages.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2022/06/2022-06-27_17-22-02.png) 

 Next, go back to the previous "Passwords & Accounts" screen. You'll see "Google" listed under "Passwords." Tap it.

![Select "Google."](https://static1.howtogeekimages.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2022/06/2022-06-27_17-24-26.png)

![Password Checkup tool.](https://static1.howtogeekimages.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2022/06/2022-06-27_17-27-58.png) 

 Lastly, we'll take a peek at the settings. Tap the gear icon in the top right corner.

![Tap the gear icon.](https://static1.howtogeekimages.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2022/06/2022-06-27_17-28-40.png) 

 Here's where you can decide if you want Google to offer to save passwords when you enter them in apps and websites in Chrome. You can choose to automatically sign in to websites and get alerts when your passwords have been compromised.
