## Mastering the Benefits of Reading Mode: A Comprehensive Guide for Android Users

"[Reading Mode](https://win-forum.techidaily.com/resolving-the-class-not-registered-issue-in-windows-environments/)" is a feature often found in web browsers that can simplify a page into a distraction-free layout. Thanks to Google's official "Reading Mode" app, you can do this pretty much anywhere on your Android device. It's pretty cool but easy to overlook tool.

##  What Does Reading Mode Do?

 Reading Mode essentially takes long content—like an editorial or a review—and puts it into a format that's easier to read. The page is stripped down to the bare essentials, and you're left with something that looks more like an ebook. Like an ebook, you can even adjust the font size, spacing, and color, as well as the background color.

 While long content is perhaps the most useful scenario for Reading Mode, it can be used on anything you'd like to read—long or short. But that's not all it's good for, either. It can also read pages out loud, with options for reading speed and language. This makes Reading Mode a [handy tool for the visually impaired](https://video-screen-grab.techidaily.com/new-2024-approved-from-play-to-pause-save-games-via-obs/) and those who have difficulty reading as well. So let's take it for a spin.

##  How to Use Reading Mode on Android

 To get started, download the official [Reading Mode app](https://www.anrdoezrs.net/links/3607085/type/dlg/sid/UUhtgUeUpU904564/https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.google.android.accessibility.reader) from the Google Play Store and open it. Tap "Continue" to proceed.

![Click "Continue" to proceed.](https://static1.howtogeekimages.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2023/07/2023-07-24_16-51-35-1.jpg) 

 The first thing we need to do is turn on the Reading Mode shortcut. This is what allows you to use the app from anywhere on your device. Tap "Settings" to continue.

![Tap "Settings."](https://static1.howtogeekimages.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2023/07/2023-07-24_16-52-38.jpg) 

 You'll be taken to the Android Accessibility settings. Find "Reading Mode" from the list of apps and make sure "Reading Mode Shortcut" is turned on.

![Enable "Reading Mode Shortcut."](https://static1.howtogeekimages.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2023/07/2023-07-24_16-53-12.jpg)
